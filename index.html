Tic-Tac-Toe

Welcome to Tic-Tac-Toe! Enjoy your game!

Here are the basic rules for playing Tic-Tac-Toe:

1. The game is played on a 3x3 grid. The objective is to be the first player to place three of their marks (X or O) in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row.

2. There are two players in the game, traditionally denoted as X and O. Player One takes X and the other takes O.

3. The game begins with an empty 3x3 grid

4. Players take turns placing their mark (X or O) on an empty cell of the grid. The player with the mark X usually goes first.

5. Players can only place their mark in an empty cell. Once a cell is marked, it cannot be changed.

6. A player wins if they have three of their marks in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. If a player achieves this, the game is over, and that player wins.

7. If all cells on the grid are filled and no player has achieved a winning combination, the game ends in a draw.

8. The game ends when either a player wins or the game ends in a draw.
